Pallet Flow Racking

Pallet flow racking, also known as gravity flow racking, is a high-density storage system designed to provide efficient pallet rotation using a First-In, First-Out (FIFO) method.

Inventory Method
FIFO flow for time-sensitive and perishable products
Movement
Inclined rollers or wheeled tracks move pallets by gravity
Storage Density
Deep-lane configuration with minimized aisle space

Features

  • FIFO inventory management keeps the first pallet loaded as the first pallet unloaded
  • High-density storage maximizes space by using depth efficiently
  • Gravity-powered movement reduces dependence on powered equipment
  • Suitable for a wide range of pallet sizes and types
  • Improves loading and unloading speed for better productivity
  • Enhances inventory control and helps reduce product obsolescence
  • Reduces forklift entry into racking lanes, improving safety
  • Ideal for fast-moving inventory and time-sensitive goods

Pallet flow racking built for FIFO rotation and gravity-powered efficiency

Pallet flow racking uses inclined rollers or wheeled tracks to move pallets from the loading end to the unloading end through gravity, making it a highly efficient system for FIFO inventory management. It is especially effective for warehouses that handle fast-moving stock and products with time-sensitive rotation requirements.

By increasing storage density, improving loading and unloading speed, and reducing the need for forklifts to enter the racking structure, pallet flow racking delivers strong space utilization, safer handling, and better inventory control across demanding warehouse environments.
